William Osborne Boats

Celebrating the craftsmanship and legacy of Littlehampton’s historic boatbuilder

History of William Osborne Ltd

William Osborne Ltd, based in Littlehampton, Sussex, was a renowned British boatbuilder from the early 20th century through the 1970s. Known for elegant wooden motor yachts and dependable naval launches, Osborne's vessels combined skilled craftsmanship with timeless design.

During WWII, Osborne built launches and patrol boats for the Admiralty. After the war, it returned to civilian yacht building, producing beloved classes like the Eagle, Falcon, and Swallow. A number of its vessels are now preserved on the National Historic Ships Register and by the Association of Dunkirk Little Ships.

The company also collaborated with Thornycroft to integrate advanced propulsion systems in military and survey vessels, including post-war refits such as HMS Medusa.

Notable Osborne Vessels

Gerfalcon (1937)

A 34ft Falcon-class cruiser, requisitioned in 1940 and served at Dunkirk. Now under restoration and featured on this site.

Aquabelle (1939)

45ft Eagle-class yacht, served in Operation Dynamo. Restored and now based at Windsor Marina.

Helinda (1939)

Originally launched as White Ghost II, she served as a patrol boat during WWII. Now privately maintained.

Naiad Errant (1939)

32ft Swallow-class cruiser, participated in Dunkirk. Carefully restored and active in ADLS events.

Albaquila (1947)

Post-war 47ft twin-screw cruiser, well preserved and restored between 2008–2013.

HMS Medusa (HDML 1387)

Originally built in 1943, refitted by Osborne in 1946. A D-Day veteran and now a fully operational historic vessel maintained by the Medusa Trust.

RNLI Lifeboats

  • Sir William Arnold (1973) – Arun-class lifeboat with over 500 launches. Preserved in Portsmouth.
  • Mary Gabriel (1973) – Rother-class, saved 44 lives. Privately owned in Queenborough.
  • The Manchester Unity of Oddfellows (1961) – Oakley-class, preserved at Sheringham.
  • J G Graves of Sheffield (1957) – Oakley-class, on display at Chatham Dockyard.
  • Silver Jubilee (1977) – Rother-class, now in New York, USA.
